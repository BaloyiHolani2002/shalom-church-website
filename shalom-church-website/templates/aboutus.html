About Shalom Church

Know our story, our purpose, and what we stand for as a community of faith.

Our History

Shalom Ministry Church was founded in 2010 with a small group of believers gathering in a home in Gawula, Giyani. Led by Pastor Maswanganyi Ms, the church started with a vision to bring God's peace (Shalom) to a broken world. What began as a prayer meeting grew into a vibrant congregation, and in 2013 we moved to our current location next to Solani Primary School. Over the years, we have seen countless lives transformed through the power of the Gospel, community outreach, and discipleship. Today, Shalom Church stands as a beacon of hope, truth, and wholeness in the region.

2010 – First home fellowship with few members
2013 – Land purchased and first church building erected
2015 – Youth and worship ministries established
2020 – Launch of online services reaching thousands
2025 – Growing as a lighthouse for true Christian faith

Vision & Mission

Vision: To see every person experience Shalom – the complete peace, wholeness, and restoration that comes from knowing Jesus Christ. We envision a community transformed by God's Word, raising disciples who impact their world.

Mission: We exist to:

  • 📖 Preach the uncompromised truth of the Bible
  • 🙌 Worship God in spirit and in truth
  • 🤝 Love and serve our local community
  • 🌍 Send and support missionaries worldwide
  • 👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Equip families and raise godly generations

"Seek the peace (Shalom) of the city where I have sent you..." – Jeremiah 29:7

What We Believe

The Bible

We believe the Bible is God's inspired Word, our final authority for faith and life.

One God

There is one God, eternally existing as Father, Son (Jesus), and Holy Spirit.

Salvation through Jesus

Jesus died for our sins, rose again, and offers eternal life to all who believe.

Holy Spirit

The Holy Spirit lives in believers, empowers us, and guides us into truth.

Love & Grace

We are saved by grace through faith, not by works, and called to love God and neighbor.

The Church

The Church is the body of Christ on earth, called to worship, fellowship, and mission.
